REM Sleep

  • REM Sleep is the short–form of rapid eye movement. Your first thought must have been — "Why will our eyes move while we sleep," right?
    • To clarify that, let us dig deep, there are four stages of sleep – wake sleep (Stage 1), light sleep (Stage 2), slow wave or deep sleep (Stage 3), REM sleep (Stage 4) - And the cycle continues so.


  • REM is the "mentally restorative" stage of sleep during which your brain converts short-term memories made during the day into long-term ones.
  • During this period or more correctly, waves, breathing becomes rapid and shallow. Your eyes move rapidly under your eyelids. Heart rate increases and blood pressure rises.
  • Because of the rapidly moving eyes under our eyelids during each cycle, these periods are known as Rapid Eye Movement Sleep.
  • We usually dream during the REM stage, that is why this word pops up almost everywhere.
